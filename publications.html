Research interests

My general area of interest is differential geometry and its applications to mathematical physics and dynamical systems. Differential geometry provides powerful and elegant techniques to characterize the symmetries, integrability, constraints and other aspects of dynamical systems. Moreover, it provides numerical methods, the so-called geometric integrators, which preserve properties (e.g., conservation of energy or conservation of the symplectic form) of the exact system. More specifically, I'm interested in the geometrical aspects of dissipative systems.

Related topics include:

  • Symplectic, contact and Poisson geometry
  • Integrable systems
  • Geometric integrators
  • Geometric control theory
  • Lie groupoids and Lie algebroids
